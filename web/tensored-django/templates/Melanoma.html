Overview

Melanoma, a type of skin cancer originating in melanocytes, the pigment-producing cells, is known for its potential to metastasize, making early detection crucial. This aggressive cancer often presents as an irregularly shaped mole or dark lesion on the skin, with a tendency to evolve in size, color, or texture.

Causes and Risk Factors

  • Excessive sun exposure
  • Sunburns
  • Fair skin
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Numerous moles
  • Weakened immune function

Symptoms

Symptoms of melanoma include the development of new moles or changes in existing ones. Pay attention to irregular borders, variations in color, diameter exceeding 6 millimeters, and evolution over time.

Treatment

Treatment options for melanoma include surgical excision, lymph node biopsy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and chemotherapy, depending on the stage and extent of the disease. Early-stage melanomas are often curable with surgical removal.

Prevention

Preventing melanoma involves sun protection practices, including sunscreen use, protective clothing, and avoiding tanning beds. Regular self-examinations and professional skin checks aid in the early identification of suspicious lesions.

How to tell moles and melanoma apart

When evaluating skin lesions, the ABCDE rule provides a framework for distinguishing between regular nevi (moles) and potential melanomas. Asymmetry, where melanomas often exhibit irregular shapes compared to the symmetrical nevi, serves as an initial consideration. Border irregularity is assessed by examining whether the lesion has uneven or notched edges, with melanomas tending to have irregular borders, while nevi typically maintain smooth contours. Color variation is another key factor; melanomas may display multiple colors, whereas nevi usually present a uniform color. Diameter, with melanomas often exceeding 6 millimeters in size, and Evolution or Change, involving alterations in size, shape, or color over time, are additional aspects to observe. Regularly applying these criteria during self-examinations can aid in the early detection of potential melanomas, prompting timely medical evaluation for further assessment.
